Design by Contract

A concept originated by the creator of Eiffel, capturing the idea that interfaces should be metaphorically like contracts between the provider and the consumer of a function. This amounts often to the provider conditionally satisfying a specification based on some characteristic of the caller.

This is a useful principle, but not taken far enough by Eiffel itself.

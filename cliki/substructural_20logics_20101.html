Substructural Logics 101

A Learning Lounge course.

  • Entry in Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.
  • The book An introduction to Substructural Logics seems to be the only good introductory resource to the whole field together. There are, though, lots of introductions online to special substructural logics (for example linear logic; see Linearity 101).
  • Relevant and Substructural Logics, an history of relevant and substructural logics, written for the Handbook of the History and Philosophy of Logic, edited by Dov Gabbay and John Woods.
